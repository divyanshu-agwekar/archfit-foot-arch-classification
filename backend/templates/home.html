Understanding Foot Arch Types

! Flat Foot (Pes Planus)

Arch absent or very low. Affects 20–30% of the population. Can cause pain and joint issues if not properly managed with supportive footwear.

✓ Normal Foot

Healthy arch distributes weight evenly, absorbs shock effectively, and supports proper posture. Optimal for overall foot health and mobility.

Problems Caused by Flat Feet

Understanding the impact of untreated flat foot conditions

Foot Pain

Chronic pain in the arch and heel areas.

Knee Stress

Increased pressure on knee joints.

Lower Back Pain

Misalignment can affect spinal health.

Poor Posture

Body compensation may lead to poor posture.

Joint Inflammation

Excessive stress may cause inflammation.

Abnormal Gait

Walking pattern can become irregular.

Flat Foot vs Normal Foot Comparison

Feature Flat Foot Normal Foot
Arch Height Absent or very low Moderate, visible curve
Weight Distribution Uneven, concentrated Evenly distributed
Shock Absorption Poor, limited capacity Excellent natural cushioning
Pain Areas Arch, heel, ankle, knee Minimal to none
Recommended Shoes Motion control, stability Neutral, flexible

Footwear Recommendations Guide

FOR FLAT FOOT

Recommended Footwear

  • ✓ Motion Control Shoes
  • ✓ Stability Shoes
  • ✓ Maximum Cushioning
  • ✓ Arch Support Insoles
Avoid: Minimalist shoes, high heels, and footwear with no arch support.
FOR NORMAL FOOT

Recommended Footwear

  • ✓ Neutral Running Shoes
  • ✓ Moderate Cushioning
  • ✓ Flexible Soles
  • ✓ Standard Athletic Shoes
Avoid: Heavy motion control shoes unless recommended by a specialist.
